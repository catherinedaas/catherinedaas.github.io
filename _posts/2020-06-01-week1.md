I am formally starting work on my DREAM project on June 2, 2026, at 7:00 a.m. sharp. Fortunately, a power outage means I have already encountered my first deviation from the plan. This deviation was easily managed with a fully charged laptop and a hotspot. May all problems be so easily overcome.

What has been accomplished so far:

1. Prior to the first week, I researched different containers, container images, and directory mounting structures to develop a plan for using AI in my workflow.
2. I clarified some of the security requirements for my research project, particularly those related to using AI in chat windows and CLI models.
3. I began reviewing technical documents related to the ongoing research project, including a preliminary review of the methods, results, and discussion from the balance board work that supported the successful completion of the Phase I SBIR award.

Impressions:

I am excited to learn something new and clinically interesting. One finding that stood out to me was that therapists working with populations at risk for age-related falls are dissatisfied with current in-clinic balance assessments, while also being resistant to moving away from known and trusted tools. This resonated with me, and it also struck me as an exciting challenge. There is room for new and better measures, but they need to be effective enough to earn clinicians’ trust.
